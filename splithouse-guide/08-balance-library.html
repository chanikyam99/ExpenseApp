Part 08

Balance Library

The core math that fixes Tricount's problem — pairwise balance calculation with exact arithmetic and a debt-simplification algorithm.

Why pairwise calculation?

Most expense apps compute each person's "net balance" by adding up what they paid and subtracting what they owe. Over time, floating-point rounding makes this drift (e.g. 0.1 + 0.2 = 0.30000000000000004).

This library calculates each pair of people independently. For any pair (A, B):

  1. Look at every expense: if B paid and A was in the split, A owes B that split amount
  2. If A paid and B was in the split, reduce what A owes B
  3. Subtract any settlements where A already paid B

Rounding only happens at the very end — never accumulated. Result is always exact to 2 decimal places.

Equal split with exact arithmetic

A ₹100 expense split 3 ways: 33.33 + 33.33 + 33.33 = 99.99 ❌ (1 paisa lost). The fix: work in integer paise, then assign the remainder to the first member:
